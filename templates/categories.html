Dataset Categories

Below are the variables that can be used to filter through our dataset, along with a brief description of each.

    Tempo - Measures the speed of a track in beats per minute.

    Danceability - A ranking of how fit a song is for dancing, using metrics such as tempo, rhythm stability, and beat strength.

    Valence - The overall emotion of a song. High valence represents happy songs, while low valence song tend to sound sad.

    Duration - Length of a song in milliseconds.

    Energy - A measure of the activity and intensity of a song.

    Loudness - The overall volume of a song in decibels.

    Liveness - Likeliness of a song being performed live, as measured by audience presence.

    Artist - Name of the person who released the song.

    Time Signature - The total duration of the all of the notes in the bar.

    Speechiness - Represents the frequency of spoken words.

    Track Album Name - Name of the album in which the song is featured.

    Track Name - Name of the song.

    Release Date - Date of release of a song.

    Instrumentalness - A measure of the how much a song relies on instrumentation. A score of 1 represents a song with no vocals.

    Key - The musical key of a song, scored 0 to 11, mirroring standard pitch class notation.

    Acousticness - A ranking of how acoustic a song is(0-1). Values closer to 1 are more acoustic.
